I Cannot Stop Myself From Buying Records

The Blow, Lee Morgan, Reading Rainbow, and more

The Blow
Poor Aim: Love Songs
This is my new favorite record to listen to super loudly! It's super slickly produced, but still mega fun because it's crunchy, poppy, and soft-hearted, and not over done.

Bobby Birdman
Giraffes and Jackals
This was partly produced by the guy who produced Poor Aim, but this is a lot less fun. It's croony and proggy and not as touching as Poor Aim.

Reading Rainbow
Prism Eyes
Fast and lo fi, but neither screechy or preachy punk. The bright noisiness doesn't bleach the color out either. It's an earnest and well-captured achievement of sounding young.

Joseph Shabason
Anne
A very personal project - this saxaphonist composed these Wyndham Hill-like ambient jazz around monologues of his mom describing her traumas. Demands emotional concentration and rewards with relaxation.

Lee Morgan
Indeed!
I love this! On a Lee Morgan jag lately. This is his debut. This record, and his other early bandleader records, I guess, are great lessons on the bop's origins in soul music. I love how this album careens that between the two.

Mix Mup
Beach Hotel De Haan
I forgot that Mix Mup isn't a Kassem Mosse alias. It seemed totally plausible that this is Kassem Mosse actually making club tracks. And it's awesome! I wish he would. Or that I should get some more Mix Mup records.
